EU Plan to Tackle Climate Change

The European Union has big goal to tackle and become Carbon Neutral also by 2050 reducing greenhouse gas emissions by 90% and get a third of its energy from renewable sources. All that cost a lot of money and even if all their states deliver on this plan which already are behind and keeping to the plan EU will have to spend 575 billion euros a year for two decades to reach their target. Its also a big ask for the pro green countries like Germany, and are deficit scared but are committed to balance the budget. EU is formalizing a plan to become Carbon Neutral by 2050 includes:

a) – Extend the EU emission trading system which lets the market to trade allowances for emissions.

b) – Consider a carbon tax on all imports so companies to not relocate to countries with easier climate roles.

c) – To turn the European Investment Bank into a “Climate Bank”, and to unlock $1.12 trillions all needed to entice private companies to spend their own capital. All this means that EU to become Carbon Neutral by 2050 must face big changes having big goals, big money, and to face the big distance towards success.
